Why Map Quiz Results to CRM Fields?

Before GoHighLevel added native quiz mapping, you had limited options: use generic tags, export data manually, or rely on third-party integrations. None of these are reliable at scale. Tags get messy. Manual work doesn't scale. Third-party tools introduce complexity and points of failure.

When you map quiz results directly to custom CRM fields, you gain:

  • Structured Data: Quiz answers live in dedicated fields, not scattered across tags or notes.
  • Instant Segmentation: Automatically segment leads based on quiz responses—no manual tagging required.
  • Smarter Automation: Build workflows that respond to specific quiz answers, not generic tags.
  • Better Personalization: Your follow-up emails, SMS, and calls reference actual quiz data—not guesses.
  • Cleaner Reporting: See which quiz answers correlate with conversions, and refine your funnel accordingly.
  • Reduced Manual Work: One less thing for your team to handle manually—data flows automatically.

In short: mapping quiz results to CRM fields transforms quiz responses from marketing data into actionable sales intelligence.

How Quiz-to-CRM Mapping Works in GoHighLevel

GoHighLevel's quiz-to-CRM mapping feature is built on a simple concept: connect quiz questions to custom CRM fields at the contact level. When a prospect completes your quiz, their answers automatically populate the corresponding fields in their contact record.

Here's the flow:

  1. You create a quiz with specific questions.
  2. You define custom CRM fields to store quiz answers (e.g., "Budget Range," "Primary Pain Point," "Timeline").
  3. You map each quiz question to its corresponding CRM field.
  4. When a prospect submits the quiz, their answers automatically populate those CRM fields.
  5. Your automations, workflows, and reports can now reference real quiz data.

The result is a contact record that contains not just basic information, but rich context about their needs, preferences, and fit for your product or service.

Step-by-Step: Setting Up Quiz Result Mapping

Step 1: Create Your Custom CRM Fields

Navigate to your GoHighLevel CRM settings and create the custom fields where quiz responses will live. For example:

  • "Primary Pain Point" (text/dropdown)
  • "Budget Range" (dropdown with tiers)
  • "Implementation Timeline" (dropdown)
  • "Current Tool Stack" (text)
  • "Decision Maker" (yes/no)

Step 2: Create or Open Your Quiz

In your GoHighLevel dashboard, go to Funnels → Quizzes and either create a new quiz or open an existing one. Ensure your quiz questions are clearly worded and align with the CRM fields you've created.

💡 Pro Tip

Name your quiz questions to match your CRM field names. This makes mapping intuitive and reduces errors. For example, if your CRM field is "Budget Range," name the quiz question exactly that.

Step 3: Access the Quiz Results Mapping Section

Within your quiz builder, look for the "Map Results" or "CRM Field Mapping" section. This is typically found in the quiz settings or results configuration area. GoHighLevel will display all available custom CRM fields here.

Step 4: Map Each Quiz Question to a CRM Field

For each question in your quiz, select the corresponding CRM field from the dropdown. For example:

  • Quiz Question: "What's your biggest challenge?" → CRM Field: "Primary Pain Point"
  • Quiz Question: "What's your budget?" → CRM Field: "Budget Range"
  • Quiz Question: "When do you need this?" → CRM Field: "Implementation Timeline"

Step 5: Save and Publish

Once all mappings are configured, save your quiz settings. Publish the quiz to your funnel, landing page, or campaign. From this point forward, all responses will automatically populate the mapped CRM fields.

Step 6: Test the Mapping

Take your own quiz with test data. Submit it, then check the contact record to confirm the answers appear in the correct CRM fields. This prevents surprises when real leads start coming in.

Real-World Automation Examples

Once your quiz results are mapped to CRM fields, the real power emerges through automation. Here are practical examples:

Example 1: Segment by Budget and Route to Sales

Create a workflow that checks the "Budget Range" field. If the value is "$50K+," immediately assign the lead to your enterprise sales team and send a personalized email acknowledging their budget tier. If it's "$5K-$25K," route to your mid-market team.

Example 2: Personalize Email Sequences Based on Pain Points

Use the "Primary Pain Point" field to personalize your email sequences. If a lead selected "Lead Generation," send them case studies about lead generation. If they selected "Sales Efficiency," send case studies about sales workflow optimization.

Example 3: Trigger Follow-Ups Based on Timeline

Check the "Implementation Timeline" field. If someone is ready to implement in 30 days, send them a faster onboarding schedule. If they're 6+ months out, send them long-term planning guides.

Example 4: Conditional SMS Outreach

Build an SMS workflow that fires immediately after quiz completion, with messaging based on quiz results. For example: "Thanks for completing the quiz! Based on your answer about [pain point], we've reserved a 1-on-1 consultation slot for you."

Best Practices for Mapping Quiz Data

Keep Questions and Fields Aligned

The simpler the connection between quiz questions and CRM fields, the fewer errors you'll encounter. Avoid mapping one question to multiple fields or vice versa.

Use Consistent Answer Formatting

If your CRM field is a dropdown, ensure your quiz question offers the same answer options in the same order. This prevents mismatched data.

Test Before Going Live

Always test your mapping with dummy submissions before launching to real prospects. Catch errors early.

Document Your Mappings

Keep a spreadsheet showing which quiz questions map to which CRM fields. This is invaluable when onboarding team members or troubleshooting later.

Review and Update Regularly

As your business evolves, your quiz and CRM fields will too. Review mappings quarterly to ensure they're still aligned with your sales process.

Troubleshooting Common Mapping Issues

Issue: Quiz Responses Aren't Appearing in CRM Fields

Solution: First, confirm that your CRM field exists and is properly configured. Then, verify that the mapping is saved and the quiz is published. Test with a fresh submission to confirm the data flows correctly.

Issue: Data Appears in the Wrong Field

Solution: Check your mapping configuration. It's easy to accidentally map Question A to Field B instead of Field A. Review the mapping one question at a time and correct any mismatches.

Issue: Quiz Responses Are Tagged But Not Mapped to Fields

Solution: Mapping and tagging are separate features. Ensure you've explicitly configured field mapping in the quiz results section—tagging alone won't populate custom fields.

Issue: Dropdown Options Don't Match Between Quiz and Field

Solution: GoHighLevel is case-sensitive and exact-match based. If your quiz offers "Yes" but your CRM field is configured for "YES," the mapping will fail. Standardize capitalization and spelling across both.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I map multiple quiz questions to the same CRM field?

No. Each CRM field should map to one quiz question for data clarity. If you need multiple questions feeding one field, restructure your quiz to have one comprehensive question instead.

What happens to CRM field data if a lead retakes the quiz?

The mapped fields will be overwritten with the new responses. If you want to preserve original quiz data, create separate fields for "Original Quiz Answers" and "Latest Quiz Answers."

Does quiz-to-CRM mapping work with conditional logic?

Yes. If your quiz has conditional branches, each path's responses will map to their corresponding CRM fields. This allows different leads to answer different questions and populate different fields based on their answers.

Can I use quiz results to trigger automations immediately?

Absolutely. Once quiz data populates your CRM fields, you can build automations triggered by field values. For example: "When Budget Range equals $100K+, send to enterprise sales team." These workflows fire instantly after quiz submission.

How do I see quiz results in reports?

Since quiz results are now in standard CRM fields, you can include them in any GoHighLevel report or dashboard. Use the CRM field filters to segment leads by quiz responses and analyze conversion rates.
