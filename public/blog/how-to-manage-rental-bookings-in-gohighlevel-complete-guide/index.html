Understanding GoHighLevel Rentals: What You're Working With

GoHighLevel's Rentals system is fundamentally different from basic appointment scheduling. It's a complete booking and inventory management platform built specifically for businesses managing physical assets—whether that's vacation rental properties, luxury vehicles, equipment, event supplies, or hospitality services.

Unlike standard appointment systems that handle single-hour bookings, Rentals in GoHighLevel supports multi-day bookings, complex pricing structures, inventory tracking, and automated payment collection. The Appointments List View sits at the heart of this system—it's your command center for viewing all booked rentals, monitoring payment statuses, and managing customer interactions in real time.

This is particularly valuable for agencies managing rental operations for multiple clients. Instead of toggling between separate systems, you manage everything from one centralized GoHighLevel dashboard.

How to Access the Appointments List View for Rental Bookings

Accessing your rental bookings dashboard is straightforward, but knowing exactly where to look saves time when you're managing dozens of properties or assets.

Step-by-step navigation:

  1. Log into your GoHighLevel account and navigate to the left sidebar menu
  2. Look for "Rentals" or "Appointments" (depending on your setup)
  3. Click on "Appointments List View" to see all your rental bookings in a unified dashboard
  4. This view displays every booked rental across all your active properties or equipment in a sortable, filterable table format

The Appointments List View shows critical information at a glance: booking dates, guest names, property/asset names, booking status, payment status, and quick-action buttons. Unlike a calendar view that shows availability, the list view is optimized for managing existing bookings and taking action on them.

💡 Pro Tip

If you manage multiple rental properties or asset types, bookmark this Appointments List View in your browser. You'll find yourself checking it dozens of times per day—having it as a quick access link saves precious minutes during peak booking periods.

Viewing and Tracking Booking Status Across All Listings

The power of the Appointments List View lies in its ability to consolidate booking information across all your listings into a single, sortable table. This is critical for rental managers who need visibility into overall occupancy and revenue.

Key information displayed in your rental bookings list:

  • Booking Dates: Check-in and check-out dates for multi-day rental periods
  • Guest Details: Customer name, contact information, and link to their profile
  • Property/Asset Name: Which rental item the booking is for—essential when managing multiple assets
  • Booking Status: Confirmed, pending, completed, or cancelled
  • Payment Status: Paid, pending, or failed—critical for revenue tracking
  • Booking Amount: Total rental price (including any fees or discounts)
  • Notes: Special requests, guest preferences, or internal notes

Sorting by payment status is particularly useful. You can instantly see which bookings have unpaid invoices, allowing you to prioritize follow-up actions. Many rental managers sort by date to see upcoming bookings or by payment status to chase down outstanding payments.

Using Filters and Sorting to Manage Appointments Efficiently

With dozens or hundreds of bookings across multiple properties, raw data becomes overwhelming fast. GoHighLevel's filtering and sorting tools let you slice your bookings into actionable segments.

Common filtering scenarios rental managers use:

  • Filter by Payment Status: Show only unpaid bookings to prioritize invoice follow-ups
  • Filter by Booking Status: View pending confirmations separately from confirmed bookings
  • Filter by Date Range: Isolate bookings for a specific week or month
  • Filter by Property/Asset: When managing multiple rental items, focus on one at a time
  • Filter by Guest Name: Useful for repeat customers or to handle special requests for specific guests

Sorting options complement filtering—sort by date to see upcoming bookings chronologically, sort by amount to identify your highest-value bookings, or sort by guest name for administrative tasks like generating mailing lists.

The combination of filters and sorting creates powerful workflows. For example: filter to show only unpaid bookings from the last 7 days, sort by amount (highest first), and you instantly see which payment follow-ups will have the biggest impact on cash flow.

Quick Actions and Editing Rental Bookings

GoHighLevel doesn't require you to open a separate detail page just to make minor booking changes. The Appointments List View includes inline quick actions for common rental management tasks.

Quick actions typically available:

  • Edit Booking: Update dates, times, guest details, or pricing
  • Mark as Paid: Confirm payment without opening the full booking record
  • Cancel Booking: Remove a booking and trigger cancellation workflows
  • Send Communication: Trigger automated SMS, email, or custom messages to the guest
  • View Guest Profile: Jump to the customer's full profile for complete history

When you need to edit a rental booking—say, a guest requests to extend their stay or change their check-in date—you can click into the booking detail view. From there, you modify dates, times, pricing, and guest information while GoHighLevel automatically updates your availability calendar and recalculates total rental costs.

Managing Time Zones for Multi-Region Rental Operations

If you manage rental properties or assets across multiple time zones, this is critical: GoHighLevel displays booking times based on the time zone setting you configure. This prevents the chaos of 3 AM check-ins or confused guests arriving at the wrong time.

Time zone best practices for rental managers:

  • Set property-specific time zones: Each rental property should use its local time zone, not your office time zone
  • Sync with guest communications: Ensure all confirmation emails and SMS messages show check-in times in the guest's local time zone
  • Account for daylight savings: GoHighLevel handles DST transitions automatically—one less thing to manage manually
  • Document your time zone settings: If multiple team members manage bookings, ensure everyone knows which time zone is displayed

This becomes especially important for vacation rentals where guests are traveling. A guest booking a mountain cabin expects to see check-in times in Mountain Time, not your headquarters' Eastern Time.

💡 Pro Tip

Many rental managers make the mistake of keeping times in their personal time zone. This leads to communication confusion and missed check-ins. Set each rental property to its local time zone from day one—GoHighLevel will display times correctly for both your team and your guests.

Best Practices for Scaling Rental Management

As you grow from managing a handful of bookings to dozens or hundreds, your approach to managing the Appointments List View needs to evolve.

Scaling strategies:

  • Create saved filter views: If your platform supports it, save frequently-used filter combinations (e.g., "Unpaid This Week" or "Pending Confirmations") for instant access
  • Delegate team responsibilities: Different team members can filter for specific properties they manage, keeping focus narrow and reducing errors
  • Monitor payment metrics: Track the ratio of pending vs. paid bookings weekly—this shows cash flow health and flags payment processing issues
  • Automate follow-ups: Use GoHighLevel workflows to automatically send payment reminders when bookings reach a certain age without payment
  • Schedule regular reviews: Set a daily or weekly review time to scan the Appointments List View for cancelled bookings or issues requiring immediate attention

Agencies reselling GoHighLevel to rental businesses often customize the Appointments List View columns and filters to match their client's specific needs—showing only the metrics that matter to that rental operation. This attention to detail often becomes a competitive differentiator.
