Understanding Prospect Status Categories in GoHighLevel

The foundation of efficient prospect management is a clear, standardized status system. GoHighLevel offers three primary prospect statuses that align with your sales pipeline: Open, Closed Won, and Closed Lost.

Open represents prospects you're actively pursuing. These are leads that have been contacted, responded positively, or are in active negotiation. They're still in your sales cycle and require follow-up.

Closed Won indicates a prospect has converted into a paying client or customer. This is the moment you move them from prospect tracking into onboarding and account management. In Agency Prospecting mode, marking a prospect Closed Won automatically enables the Create Sub-Account option—critical for agencies managing multiple client accounts.

Closed Lost marks prospects who didn't convert. This might be due to budget constraints, timing misalignment, or a competitor win. Tracking these is equally important because it helps you identify patterns and refine your outreach strategy.

💡 Pro Tip

Use Closed Lost status to tag prospects by reason (budget, timing, competitor, no response). This data reveals which objections are most common, helping your team craft better pitches and qualify faster.

How to Mark Prospects with Status (Open, Closed Won, Closed Lost)

Marking a prospect's status in GoHighLevel is straightforward and can be done in just a few clicks. Navigate to your Prospecting tool and locate the prospect you want to update. Look for the status dropdown field—it's prominently displayed and accessible directly from the prospect's record.

Click the status dropdown and select your choice: Open, Closed Won, or Closed Lost. The system saves your selection immediately, so there's no need for additional confirmation steps.

For Closed Won prospects, you'll notice an additional option appears: Create Sub-Account. This is exclusive to Agency Prospecting and allows you to instantly convert the prospect into a new client account within your agency's workspace. You can set billing information, assign team members, and begin onboarding all from this point.

For Closed Lost prospects, you have the option to add notes explaining why the prospect didn't convert. Document the reason—this helps your team learn and improves future qualification efforts.

Bulk status updates are also available. Select multiple prospects using the checkboxes and apply a status change to all of them simultaneously. This is especially useful when you're closing out a campaign or moving a batch of leads to the next stage.

Filtering and Organizing Prospects by Status

Once you're marking prospects with statuses, the real power emerges: organization at scale. GoHighLevel's filtering system lets you view only the prospects you need to focus on right now.

Use the status filter at the top of your Prospecting list to display prospects by category. You can view all Open prospects to see active pipeline, filter to see only Closed Won to celebrate wins and prepare onboarding, or isolate Closed Lost to analyze win/loss patterns.

Combining status filters with other filters—like campaign source, tag, or assigned team member—creates custom views. For example, filter for "Open AND assigned to Sarah AND from LinkedIn campaign" to see exactly which prospects Sarah needs to follow up with from a specific source.

These filtered views save time and reduce context switching. Your team sees only relevant prospects, making it easier to prioritize high-value opportunities and follow up before momentum fades.

Unlocking Create Sub-Account for Won Prospects in Agency Prospecting

One of GoHighLevel's most powerful features for agencies is the ability to create a client sub-account directly from a Closed Won prospect. This eliminates the need to manually recreate prospect data in a separate system or spreadsheet.

When you mark a prospect as Closed Won in Agency Prospecting mode, the Create Sub-Account button automatically becomes available. Click it and you're prompted to configure the new client's account: business name, billing contact, plan level, and team member assignments.

This workflow is transformative because it:

  • Eliminates data re-entry. The prospect's contact information carries forward, reducing manual data entry errors.
  • Accelerates onboarding. The client is immediately set up in your system, ready for service delivery to begin.
  • Maintains audit trail. The prospect record stays intact, showing the complete journey from initial outreach to conversion.
  • Enables fast team assignment. You can assign the new sub-account to the right team member on day one.

This is especially valuable for agencies managing 20+ clients, where manual account creation becomes a bottleneck. Every minute saved on admin work is a minute your team can spend on client delivery.

Best Practices for Managing Prospect Statuses Across Your Team

To maximize the benefit of prospect status management, establish clear team guidelines for when and how statuses are updated.

Define status transition rules. Document when a prospect moves from Open to Closed Won or Closed Lost. For example: "Mark as Closed Won only when contract is signed and first payment is received." This prevents premature celebration and ensures consistency across the team.

Assign status ownership. Decide who updates statuses. Some agencies require the prospect owner to update, while others designate a single person to review and confirm status changes. Clear ownership prevents gaps and duplicates.

Use status notes. For Closed Lost prospects, always include a brief note explaining why. This feedback loop helps your sales team continuously improve qualification and messaging.

Review status metrics weekly. Pull a status report showing how many prospects are Open, Closed Won, and Closed Lost. Track win rate over time. This data reveals pipeline health and conversion trends, informing your outreach strategy.

Automate status-based workflows. Use GoHighLevel's automation to trigger actions based on status. For example, send a Closed Won prospect an onboarding email automatically, or notify your manager when a prospect is marked Closed Lost.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I change a prospect's status back to Open after marking them Closed Won or Closed Lost?

Yes. Status changes are not permanent. If a Closed Won prospect asks to pause their service or a Closed Lost prospect re-engages, simply update the status back to Open. GoHighLevel retains the full history, so you can always see the prospect's status timeline.

What happens to the prospect record after I create a sub-account from a Closed Won status?

The prospect record remains in your Prospecting tool with Closed Won status, showing the conversion date. The new client sub-account is created as a separate entity in your agency workspace. The prospect record serves as historical documentation of how you acquired this client.

Can team members see why a prospect was marked Closed Lost?

Yes, if you add notes when closing the prospect as Lost. These notes are visible to anyone with access to the prospect record, helping your entire team learn from the outcome and refine outreach strategies.

Is there a limit to how many prospects I can filter and view at once?

No. You can filter by status and view as many prospects as match your criteria. For very large lists, GoHighLevel's interface handles pagination smoothly, ensuring performance remains fast even with thousands of prospects.

Can I create automated reports showing prospect status changes over time?

Yes. GoHighLevel includes reporting features that track prospects by status. You can generate weekly or monthly reports showing how many prospects moved through each status, win rates, and pipeline velocity—critical metrics for agency performance tracking.
