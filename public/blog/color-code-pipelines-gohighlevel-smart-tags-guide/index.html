What Are Smart Tags and Why They Matter

Smart Tags are color-coded labels that automatically highlight opportunities in your GoHighLevel pipeline based on custom rules. Instead of manually sorting deals or relying on text-only descriptions, Smart Tags give your team instant visual feedback on deal status, value, and urgency.

Think of them as intelligent automation for pipeline organization. A deal moves into a certain stage or meets a specific condition (like staying untouched for 7 days), and a colored chip appears automatically. Your sales reps don't have to think — they just see red tags for stale deals, green for hot leads, gold for high-value opportunities.

For agencies managing client pipelines, this is game-changing. You can segment accounts by value tier, track lead freshness across accounts, flag deals that need immediate attention, and keep team performance transparent — all without a single manual update.

💡 Pro Tip

Smart Tags work best when you limit yourself to 3-5 tags per pipeline. Too many tags create visual noise and defeat the purpose of quick scanning. Focus on the metrics that actually drive your business.

How to Create and Configure Smart Tags in GoHighLevel

Step 1: Navigate to Your Pipeline Settings

Log into your GoHighLevel account and go to CRM → Pipelines. Select the pipeline where you want to add Smart Tags. Click the pipeline name to open settings.

Step 2: Access the Smart Tags Panel

In the pipeline settings, look for the Smart Tags section. This is where you'll create all tags for that specific pipeline. Click Add New Tag.

Step 3: Name Your Tag and Choose a Color

Give your tag a clear, descriptive name — "Hot Lead," "Stale Deal," "VIP Account," etc. Choose a color that matches the tag's purpose. Red for urgent, green for active, gold for high-value. Consistency matters because your team will learn to scan by color.

Step 4: Set Tag Visibility

Decide if this tag appears as a colored chip in the pipeline view. Most tags should be visible — that's the whole point. Some agencies use hidden tags for backend automation, but for 90% of cases, keep them visible.

That's it. You've created your first Smart Tag. Now comes the powerful part — automation rules.

Setting Up Automated Rules for Deal Prioritization

Creating a tag is step one. Making it appear automatically is where Smart Tags earn their name.

Define Rules Based on Deal Conditions

Click into your newly created tag and select Add Rule. GoHighLevel lets you build rules around:

  • Stage — Tag appears when an opportunity reaches a specific pipeline stage (e.g., "Negotiation" stage)
  • Custom Fields — Tag appears based on field values (deal value, lead source, account type)
  • Time-Based Triggers — Tag appears after X days of inactivity, on specific dates, or based on last activity
  • Contact/Lead Properties — Tag appears based on contact type, tags they already have, or contact owner
  • Multiple Conditions — Combine rules with AND/OR logic (e.g., "High-value AND no activity for 5 days")

Example: Stale Deal Rule

Create a tag called "Stale — 7 Days." Set the rule: Last Activity is more than 7 days ago AND Opportunity Stage is not "Closed Won." This tag appears automatically on any deal that hasn't been touched in a week, alerting your team to follow up.

Example: High-Value Opportunity Rule

Create a tag called "VIP — $50K+." Set the rule: Deal Value is greater than or equal to $50,000. Every opportunity over your threshold gets flagged instantly, helping your sales director spot which deals deserve CEO attention.

Example: New Lead Rule

Create a tag called "New Lead." Set the rule: Date Created is within the last 2 days. Fresh leads get highlighted so your team jumps on them while they're warm.

Best Practices for Managing Multiple Tags Across Pipelines

Keep Tag Names Consistent Across Pipelines

If you have multiple pipelines (Sales Pipeline, Service Pipeline, Partner Pipeline), use the same tag names and colors across them. Your team will instantly understand what a red "Urgent" tag means in any pipeline.

Use a Tag Hierarchy

Organize tags by priority:

  • Status Tags — "Active," "Stale," "Won," "Lost"
  • Value Tags — "Standard," "High-Value," "VIP"
  • Action Tags — "Needs Follow-up," "Legal Review," "Technical Approval"
  • Source Tags — "Inbound," "Referral," "Outbound"

This prevents tag sprawl and keeps your pipeline scannable.

Audit Your Tags Monthly

Check if tags are actually appearing correctly and providing value. Remove tags that never trigger or that your team ignores. Refine rules that are too broad (tagging 50% of deals) or too narrow (tagging 1% of deals).

Train Your Team on Tag Meanings

Document what each tag means and what action it requires. A "Stale" tag should trigger a follow-up email. A "Legal Review" tag should trigger an assignment to your legal person. Without clarity, tags become noise.

Editing, Deleting, and Using Pre-Built Tag Templates

Editing Existing Tags

Go back to your pipeline's Smart Tags panel, click the tag you want to modify, and update its name, color, or rules. Changes apply instantly across all opportunities that match the rule — no manual re-tagging needed.

Deleting Tags You Don't Use

If a tag no longer serves your team, simply delete it from the Smart Tags panel. This doesn't affect historical data; it just stops the tag from appearing on new deals. If you want to preserve the tag but stop it from appearing, disable its rules instead.

Leveraging Pre-Built Tag Templates

GoHighLevel includes pre-built Smart Tag templates for common use cases:

  • Sales Pipeline templates (Hot Lead, Warm Lead, Cold Lead, Stale)
  • Agency Service templates (Discovery Phase, Proposal Sent, Onboarding, Upsell)
  • Lead Scoring templates (High Intent, Medium Intent, Low Intent)

Start with a template and customize the rules to match your business. This saves time and ensures best practices.

Real-World Smart Tag Examples for Agencies

Digital Marketing Agency Pipeline

Create tags for: "Proposal Pending" (red), "Contract Signed" (green), "Onboarding Started" (blue), "Upsell Ready" (gold). Use rules based on pipeline stage and activity to keep your entire team aligned on which accounts need immediate action.

Software Development Agency

Create tags for: "Discovery Call Booked" (green), "Quote Needed" (red), "Waiting on Client Response" (yellow), "Budget Approved" (gold). Rules can be based on deal stage, custom fields tracking approval status, and days since last contact.

Agency with Multiple Client Accounts

Create tags for: "Requires Account Manager Review" (red), "Strategic Account — $100K ARR+" (gold), "New Service Opportunity" (green), "At-Risk Contract" (red). Rules trigger based on deal value, age, and custom risk scoring fields you've built into your CRM.

The key is matching tags to your actual sales motion. If your biggest bottleneck is late-stage deal follow-up, create tags around that. If it's lead qualification, focus your tags there.

Smart Tags are the fastest way to eliminate pipeline chaos. They replace manual sorting with intelligent automation, give your team instant visual feedback, and ensure nothing falls through the cracks. Set them up correctly once, and they work for you forever. Start with 3-4 tags that solve your biggest pipeline problem, test your rules, and expand from there. Your sales team will thank you.
