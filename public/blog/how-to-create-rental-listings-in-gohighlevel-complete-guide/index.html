Understanding GoHighLevel's Rentals Feature

Before diving into the technical setup, it's important to understand what GoHighLevel's Rentals feature actually does and why it matters for your business.

GoHighLevel's Rentals is a complete booking and inventory management system built into your CRM. It handles:

  • Listing creation and management — publish professional rental listings with images, descriptions, and specifications
  • Real-time availability tracking — sync calendars and prevent double bookings automatically
  • Dynamic pricing — set different rates for different seasons, rental durations, or property types
  • Booking automation — clients can book directly, payment is collected, and confirmations are sent automatically
  • Inventory variants — manage multiple versions of the same rental (color, size, model) with separate pricing

This eliminates the need for separate rental software, spreadsheets, or third-party booking platforms. Everything lives in one system, which means better data, fewer errors, and faster scaling.

How to Access and Create a New Rental Listing

Getting started with your first rental listing is straightforward. Here's exactly how to do it:

Step 1: Navigate to the Rentals Section

From your GoHighLevel dashboard, look for the Rentals module in your left sidebar. Click on it to enter the Rentals interface. If you don't see it, make sure your subscription tier includes the Rentals feature (available on higher-tier plans).

Step 2: Click "Create New Listing"

In the Rentals section, you'll see a button labeled "Create New Listing" or "+ Add Listing." Click it to open the quick-create modal.

Step 3: Fill in Basic Information

The modal will prompt you to enter:

  • Listing Title — be clear and descriptive (e.g., "3-Bedroom Beach House" or "Toyota Camry 2023")
  • Category — select from Properties, Vehicles, Equipment, or custom categories
  • Description — highlight key features, amenities, or specifications
  • Location/Address — where the rental is based or will be delivered from
  • Base Price — starting rental rate (you'll configure variants and rules later)

Step 4: Upload Images

Add high-quality photos of your rental. GoHighLevel supports multiple images per listing. Professional photos significantly increase conversion rates, so invest time here.

Once you've filled in these basics, click "Create" to move to the detailed configuration screen.

Configuring Inventory and Variants for Your Rentals

This is where you customize your listing to match your exact rental business model.

Setting Up Inventory

In the Inventory section, you'll specify how many units of this rental you have available. This is critical for preventing overbooking:

  • Set your Total Quantity (e.g., if you own 5 Toyota Camrys, enter 5)
  • Choose whether to track by individual item or total pool
  • If tracking individually, name each unit for better organization

Adding Variants

Variants allow you to offer different versions of the same rental with separate pricing. For example:

  • Vehicle rental: Create variants for "Economy," "Midsize," and "SUV"
  • Property rental: Create variants for "Studio," "1-Bedroom," and "2-Bedroom"
  • Equipment rental: Create variants for "Basic," "Professional," and "Premium" packages

To add a variant, click "+ Add Variant" in the Variants section. You can assign different:

  • Pricing per day/week/month
  • Inventory quantities
  • Feature descriptions
  • Images (optional)

💡 Pro Tip

Use variants strategically to increase average order value. Instead of just offering one property, create variants for different room counts or amenity levels. Customers will often upgrade to a higher variant when given the option.

Setting Up Pricing Rules and Booking Options

Dynamic Pricing

GoHighLevel allows you to set different prices based on rental duration and seasonality. This is essential for maximizing revenue:

  • Daily Rate: Cost per night/day
  • Weekly Discount: Reduced rate for 7+ day bookings
  • Monthly Rate: Even lower rate for 30+ day bookings
  • Seasonal Pricing: Higher rates during peak season, lower during off-season

To configure pricing rules, go to the Pricing tab in your listing. You can create multiple price sets and assign them to specific date ranges. For example, charge 2x your normal rate during holidays or summer vacation season.

Booking Options

Configure how customers interact with your rental:

  • Rental Duration: Allow hourly, daily, weekly, or monthly bookings
  • Minimum Stay: Require a minimum number of days (e.g., 3-day minimum)
  • Maximum Stay: Cap rental duration
  • Lead Time: How many hours/days in advance customers must book
  • Deposit: Collect a refundable or non-refundable deposit upfront
  • Cleaning Fee: Add a flat fee or percentage-based cleaning charge
  • Cancellation Policy: Set refund terms (full refund, partial refund, non-refundable)

Managing Calendar Synchronization and Availability

Your booking calendar is the backbone of your rental business. GoHighLevel offers powerful calendar management tools:

Built-In Calendar View

Each listing has its own calendar showing booked and available dates. You can:

  • See all bookings at a glance
  • Manually block off dates for maintenance or personal use
  • View confirmed, pending, and cancelled bookings
  • Adjust availability in real-time

Multi-Calendar Sync

If you manage rentals across multiple platforms (Airbnb, VRBO, etc.), you can sync those calendars to prevent double bookings. GoHighLevel allows you to:

  • Connect external calendar feeds via iCal URL
  • Push availability to connected platforms
  • Block dates that are booked elsewhere automatically

💡 Pro Tip

Always sync your GoHighLevel calendar with external platforms if you list on multiple sites. Use the iCal feed feature to keep availability updated automatically and avoid the nightmare of double bookings.

Publishing and Previewing Your Rental Listings

Before going live, make sure everything is configured correctly:

Preview Your Listing

Click the "Preview" button to see exactly how customers will see your rental listing. Check:

  • Image quality and layout
  • Pricing display and clarity
  • Description accuracy and completeness
  • Variant options presentation
  • Booking calendar functionality

Publish Your Listing

Once you're satisfied, click "Publish" to make your listing live. Your rental will now:

  • Appear in your online booking portal
  • Be available for customer bookings
  • Show real-time availability based on your calendar
  • Accept payments automatically via GoHighLevel's payment processing

You can unpublish a listing at any time by clicking "Unpublish" if you need to remove it temporarily.

Best Practices for Rental Management in GHL

1. Use Detailed Descriptions

Don't skimp on description text. Include amenities, dimensions, condition, what's included, and what's not. Detailed listings reduce booking friction and minimize cancellations due to unmet expectations.

2. Set Conservative Availability

Always leave buffer days between bookings for cleaning, maintenance, and turnover. A blocked day is better than a bad review from a customer who received a dirty rental.

3. Monitor and Adjust Pricing Regularly

Use GoHighLevel's booking analytics to track occupancy rates and revenue. If a variant is always booked, consider raising its price. If a variant sits empty, lower its price or discontinue it.

4. Automate Your Communication

Set up automated workflows in GoHighLevel to send booking confirmations, reminders, check-in instructions, and post-rental surveys. Automation saves time and improves customer experience.

5. Keep Your Calendar Synchronized

If you use multiple booking platforms, sync your calendars religiously. One double booking can cost you thousands in lost revenue and reputation damage.

6. Leverage Customer Data

GoHighLevel is a CRM first. Use booking data to segment customers, run marketing campaigns, and build a loyalty program. Your rental customers are valuable repeat-business opportunities.
